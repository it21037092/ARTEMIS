Project Scope

General Introduction

Artillery fire remains one of the most pivotal elements in modern military operations, forming the backbone of strategic offensive and defensive maneuvers. Historically, the mastery of artillery deployment required extensive live-fire exercises, which, while effective, are increasingly hindered by logistical constraints, safety hazards, and escalating costs. The advancement of simulation technologies—specifically Virtual Reality (VR) and Artificial Intelligence (AI)—presents a transformative opportunity to modernize traditional training paradigms.

This project introduces the Gun End component of the ARTEMIS Immersive Artillery Fire and Evaluation System, an initiative to build a virtual training environment that realistically simulates artillery gun operations, aiming, and firing using VR and AI technologies.

The Gun End is not merely a replica of an artillery system; it is a high-fidelity training tool designed to immerse military trainees in virtual scenarios that mimic real battlefield environments. With AI-assisted feedback, realistic environmental modeling, and real-time ballistics calculations, the Gun End aims to bridge the gap between theoretical training and operational readiness. The module’s design integrates the real-time data feedback loop, trajectory simulations, and a motion-based VR interface to ensure accurate replication of artillery handling.

Supported and validated by the Sri Lankan Army, the Gun End is positioned to become a benchmark in defense training innovation. The module supports interactive learning, continuous performance evaluation, and a cost-effective alternative to live ammunition drills. Its development acknowledges the increasing need for military preparedness in constrained environments and proposes a scalable, safe, and data-driven training approach.

Research Gap

  • No system combines real-time calculations, VR, and automated feedback
  • ARTEMIS fills this gap with cutting-edge solutions

Research Problem

  • Limited realistic training environments
  • Lack of real-time feedback and analysis
  • High costs & risks in live exercises

Research Objectives

Main Objective: To develop an immersive artillery fire evaluation system that enhances training through real-time impact analysis, automated feedback, and VR integration.

Specific Objectives (SMART):

  • Specific: Implement real-time calculations, VR, and automated feedback
  • Measurable: Evaluate artillery accuracy and training effectiveness
  • Achievable: Develop within 10 months
  • Relevant: Addresses limitations in training methods
  • Time-bound: Completes within final year research timeline
